3-Tab to Architectural: The Upgrade Old Brooklyn Homeowners Make

When Old Brooklyn homeowners replace aging 3-tab roofs, the vast majority choose to upgrade to architectural shingles — and for good reason. The GAF Timberline HDZ, the most popular choice, offers a Class 4 impact resistance rating (important in hail-prone Northeast Ohio), 130 mph wind resistance, and a dramatically improved dimensional appearance compared to the flat look of 3-tab. The price difference between a 3-tab replacement and an architectural upgrade is typically modest — often just a few hundred dollars on a standard Old Brooklyn home.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between 3-tab and architectural shingles?+
3-tab shingles are thin, flat, and have a uniform appearance. Architectural (dimensional) shingles are thicker, heavier, have a layered textured look, and are significantly more wind-resistant — typically rated to 130 mph vs. 60-70 mph for 3-tab. They also carry better warranties.
Should I replace 3-tab with architectural shingles in Old Brooklyn?+
Almost always yes, when it's time to replace. The cost difference is modest and the performance improvement is significant, especially in Old Brooklyn's climate where wind and storm exposure is a real factor. We walk you through the options during your free estimate.
How long do architectural shingles last?+
Quality architectural shingles like GAF Timberline HDZ are rated for 30+ years. With proper installation and the GAF System Plus Warranty, many homeowners see 25-30 years of service life in Ohio's climate.
Can I put architectural shingles over my existing 3-tab roof?+
Ohio building code generally allows one overlay, but we typically recommend full tear-off for Old Brooklyn homes. Tear-off allows us to inspect and repair the decking, properly install ice and water shield, and ensure a flat substrate — resulting in a better-looking and longer-lasting roof.
