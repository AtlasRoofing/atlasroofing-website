What Is Roofing Underlayment? Felt vs Synthetic Explained

Roofing underlayment is a water-resistant layer installed directly over the roof deck — under the shingles — that provides secondary protection if water gets past the shingles. Modern installations use synthetic underlayment (polyethylene or polypropylene) rather than traditional felt paper. Synthetic underlayment is lighter, stronger, more tear-resistant, and more water-resistant than felt. It does not absorb water and remains dimensionally stable in wet conditions. What underlayment does

Underlayment sits between your roof deck and your shingles. It serves as the second line of defense when water gets past the shingles — during wind-driven rain, at damaged shingle areas, or during the period between tear-off and installation. It also provides a temporary weather barrier during installation when the crew stops at the end of a day or is delayed by weather.

Felt paper — the old standard

Traditional roofing felt (15 lb or 30 lb) is asphalt-saturated organic felt paper. It was the industry standard for decades and is still code-compliant. However, felt has significant limitations:

  • Absorbs water when saturated — swells, wrinkles, and can slip on steep slopes when wet
  • Tears relatively easily during installation foot traffic
  • Degrades faster under UV exposure when left exposed between tear-off and installation
  • Heavier — more weight on the structure

30 lb felt is meaningfully better than 15 lb for secondary protection but still inferior to modern synthetic products.

Synthetic underlayment — the current standard

Synthetic underlayment (woven or non-woven polypropylene or polyethylene) has largely replaced felt as the industry standard for quality installations. The advantages are significant:

  • Does not absorb water — remains flat and stable when wet, won't wrinkle or slip
  • Higher tear strength — better withstands foot traffic and wind before shingles are installed
  • Lighter — easier to handle and less structural load
  • Better UV resistance — can be left exposed longer without degradation
  • Non-slip surface on most products — important for crew safety on steep slopes

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between 15 lb and 30 lb felt?
15 lb felt is lighter and thinner — the minimum code-compliant option. 30 lb felt is heavier and provides more secondary protection and better tear resistance. Neither is as good as modern synthetic underlayment in terms of water resistance, dimensional stability, or durability.
Is synthetic underlayment worth it?
Yes — synthetic underlayment outperforms felt paper in every category that matters for roof performance: water resistance, tear strength, dimensional stability when wet, and UV durability. The cost difference is modest. Any quality contractor uses synthetic as a standard, not an upgrade.
Does underlayment matter if shingles are good?
Yes — shingles are not perfectly waterproof. Wind-driven rain, ice dam backup, and any shingle damage allow water to reach the underlayment. Underlayment is the last line of defense before water reaches your roof deck. Its quality directly affects how well your roof performs under adverse conditions.
Can you re-use underlayment when re-roofing?
No — underlayment is removed with the old shingles during tear-off and must be replaced. Attempting to re-use old underlayment compromises the warranty and the secondary protection the new roof system depends on.
What underlayment does GAF require for the System Plus warranty?
GAF requires the use of qualifying GAF accessories — including GAF-branded underlayment — as part of the full GAF roofing system for System Plus warranty eligibility. GAF FeltBuster or equivalent GAF synthetic underlayment satisfies this requirement.
