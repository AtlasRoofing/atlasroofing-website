About modified bitumen

Why Modified Bitumen Works Well in Northeast Ohio

❄️
Cold-Weather Performance

Modified bitumen contains polymers that maintain flexibility in cold temperatures — important in Ohio where brittle membranes crack and fail during freeze-thaw cycles. It's one of the best-performing flat roof systems for Northeast Ohio winters.

💧
Outstanding Waterproofing

The multi-layer system — typically a base sheet plus cap sheet — provides multiple barriers against water intrusion. It's one of the most waterproof systems available for flat and low-slope applications.

🔧
Easy to Repair

Modified bitumen repairs are straightforward — damaged sections can be cut out and replaced with compatible material. It's a system that can be effectively maintained and extended over its lifespan.

FAQs

Common Questions

How is modified bitumen installed?+
Modified bitumen is installed in two primary methods — torch-applied (using open flame to heat-bond the membrane) and self-adhered (using a peel-and-stick membrane). We select the appropriate method based on the building type, local codes, and project requirements.
How does modified bitumen compare to TPO?+
Modified bitumen has a longer track record and excellent cold-weather performance, making it a strong choice for Ohio. TPO offers better energy efficiency and heat-welded seams. Both are good systems — the best choice depends on your building's specific needs. We'll help you compare during your free inspection.
