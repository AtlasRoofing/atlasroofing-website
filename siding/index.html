Siding materials

The Right Material for Your Home

We'll help you choose the best siding material for your home's style, your budget, and Cleveland's climate.

🏡

Vinyl Siding

Our most popular option. Vinyl siding is durable, virtually maintenance-free, and available in dozens of colors and profiles. It holds up exceptionally well to Northeast Ohio's freeze-thaw cycles and doesn't rot, warp, or need painting. Best value for most homeowners.

🪨

Fiber Cement Siding

James Hardie and similar fiber cement products offer the premium look of wood with far superior durability. Resistant to fire, moisture, insects, and impact. Ideal for homeowners who want a high-end appearance and are willing to invest in a longer-lasting product.

FAQs

Siding Questions Answered

Honest answers to the most common siding questions from Northeast Ohio homeowners.

How long does vinyl siding last?+
Quality vinyl siding typically lasts 20–40 years with minimal maintenance. Its lifespan depends on the quality of the product installed, how well it was installed, and Northeast Ohio's climate conditions. Unlike wood, vinyl doesn't rot or need repainting, which significantly reduces long-term maintenance costs.
Will my insurance cover hail-damaged siding?+
Hail and wind damage to siding is typically covered by homeowner's insurance. The key is proper documentation of the damage. We specialize in storm damage inspections — we'll photograph and document everything needed to support your insurance claim and maximize your coverage.
What's the difference between vinyl and fiber cement siding?+
Vinyl is lower cost, lighter weight, and requires no painting or staining. Fiber cement (like James Hardie) is heavier, more expensive, but offers superior impact resistance, a more premium look, and can be painted. Both are excellent choices — the right one depends on your budget and priorities. We're happy to walk you through the trade-offs.
How long does siding installation take?+
Most single-family home siding replacements take 2–5 days depending on the size of the home, the material chosen, and whether any underlying repairs are needed to the sheathing or trim. We'll give you a firm timeline before we start.
