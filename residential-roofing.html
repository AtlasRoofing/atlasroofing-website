Know the warning signs

Is It Time for a New Roof?

Most homeowners don't know their roof needs attention until there's a leak inside. Here are the warning signs to look for — and when to call us.


Not sure? Our free inspection takes 30–45 minutes and gives you a complete picture of your roof's condition with no obligation to move forward.



Book a Free Inspection
01
Missing, curling, or cracked shingles

Shingles that are curling at the edges, cracking, or missing entirely are exposing your roof deck to moisture. This often signals a roof nearing the end of its life.

02
Granules in your gutters

Asphalt shingles lose their granule coating as they age. If you're seeing dark gritty material in your gutters or downspouts, your shingles are deteriorating.

03
Water stains on ceilings or walls

Indoor water stains often point to a roof leak. Don't wait — even a small leak can cause significant structural damage and mold growth over time.

04
Roof is 15–20+ years old

Most asphalt shingle roofs have a functional lifespan of 15–25 years. If your roof is approaching or past this range, an inspection can tell you how much life is left.

05
Sagging roof deck

A sagging or uneven roofline indicates potential structural damage or a compromised roof deck. This needs attention immediately before it becomes a safety issue.

06
After a hail or windstorm

Storm damage isn't always visible from the ground. After any significant hail or wind event, a professional inspection can catch damage early — and may qualify for an insurance-covered replacement.

Wondering if insurance will cover your roof?

Storm or hail damage may qualify for a full replacement — covered by your homeowner's insurance at no out-of-pocket cost beyond your deductible. We inspect for free and handle the entire claim process for you.

Common questions

Residential Roofing FAQs

Honest answers to the questions we hear most from Cleveland-area homeowners.



See All FAQs
How do I know if I need a repair or full replacement?+
If your roof is under 15 years old with isolated damage — a few missing shingles, one leak in a specific area — a targeted repair is often the right call. If it's 15–20+ years old, has widespread shingle damage, multiple leaks, or significant storm damage across the whole surface, a full replacement is usually more cost-effective in the long run. We'll give you our honest recommendation during your free inspection. We never push a replacement when a repair will do the job.
How long does a roof replacement take?+
Most residential roof replacements are fully completed in a single day. Larger homes, complex rooflines, or roofs requiring deck replacement may take two days. We give you a firm timeline upfront so you can plan accordingly, and we don't leave jobs half-done overnight.
Can I install a new roof over my existing one?+
In most cases, we recommend a full tear-off rather than installing over an existing layer. A proper tear-off allows us to inspect and replace the roof deck if needed, ensures proper installation of underlayment and flashing, and typically results in a longer-lasting roof. Many manufacturers also require a full tear-off to honor their warranty. We'll let you know what we find during your inspection.
Will my insurance cover a new roof?+
It depends on the cause of damage. Storm damage, hail, and high winds are typically covered by homeowner's insurance. General wear and aging usually is not. We specialize in insurance claim assistance — we'll inspect your roof, document any storm or hail damage, and work with your adjuster to maximize your claim. Many of our customers end up paying only their deductible for a full roof replacement.
What roofing materials do you recommend for Cleveland?+
For most Cleveland-area homeowners, we recommend GAF architectural (dimensional) asphalt shingles. They offer an excellent balance of durability, curb appeal, cost, and warranty coverage — and they hold up well against Northeast Ohio's freeze-thaw cycles, ice dams, and hail. For homeowners looking for the longest-lasting option, metal roofing is worth considering. We'll walk you through the trade-offs during your consultation.
Do you offer financing?+
Yes. We offer flexible financing options that can make a new roof fit your budget with manageable monthly payments. Ask us about current financing options during your free inspection — we'll help you find a plan that works for you.
What does your GAF warranty cover?+
As a GAF Certified contractor, we can offer the GAF Lifetime Roofing System warranty — which covers both materials and installation for the lifetime of your home when you use the full GAF system. This is one of the best warranties available in residential roofing, and most local contractors are not certified to offer it. We'll walk you through all the details during your consultation.
