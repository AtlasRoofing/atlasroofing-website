Storm damage facts

What 2.5" Hail Does to Your Roof

Golf ball-sized hail is the threshold for severe roofing damage. At 2.5 inches, hailstones have enough mass and velocity to crack asphalt shingles, fracture fiberglass matting, and strip away the granule protective layer that prevents UV degradation and water infiltration.

  • Shingle bruising and fracturing — the most common and most missed type of damage
  • Granule displacement — check your gutters and downspouts for dark granules
  • Dented metal surfaces — gutters, flashing, HVAC units are visible indicators
  • Damaged siding — vinyl and aluminum siding show impact marks clearly
  • Cracked or broken skylights — common with hail this size

Common questions

FAQ — April 2026 Hail Storm

How do I know if my roof was damaged by today's hail storm?+
Check your gutters and downspouts for dark granules — this is the easiest visible sign. Look at your window screens and aluminum flashing for small circular dents. If hail was 2.5 inches in your area, assume your roof was impacted and schedule a free inspection. Most hail damage is not visible from the ground.
Will my insurance cover this hail storm?+
Almost certainly yes. Hail damage is a covered peril under standard Ohio homeowner's insurance policies. The key is proper documentation. Atlas inspects your roof, photographs all damage, and manages your claim from start to finish.
How quickly do I need to file a claim?+
Most Ohio homeowner's insurance policies allow 1–2 years from the storm date to file a claim. However, evidence of hail damage degrades over time — granules wash away, bruising becomes harder to distinguish. The sooner you document, the stronger your claim.
How much will I have to pay out of pocket?+
If your claim is approved, you pay your deductible — typically $500 to $2,000 depending on your policy. Atlas does not charge for the inspection, claim filing, or adjuster representation. The insurance company pays Atlas directly for the approved work.
Can I choose my own roofing contractor for an insurance claim?+
Yes — in Ohio, you have the right to choose your own licensed contractor for insurance claim work. Your insurance company cannot require you to use their preferred contractor. Atlas works directly with all major insurance carriers.
