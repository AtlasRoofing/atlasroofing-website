Quick Answer

For most Northeast Ohio homes: GAF Timberline HDZ — best balance of wind resistance, warranty, and value. For hail-prone areas: GAF Timberline UHDZ — Class 4 impact resistant, may qualify for insurance discount. Budget option: GAF Timberline NS.

GAF Timberline NS — Entry-Level Architectural

The Timberline NS (New Standard) is GAF's entry point into architectural shingles. It provides a dimensional appearance at a lower cost but lacks the LayerLock technology that makes the HDZ superior in wind events. Best for budget-conscious homeowners, investment properties, or situations where maximum wind resistance is not a priority.

GAF Timberline HDZ — The Standard Choice

The Timberline HDZ is the most installed shingle in North America. LayerLock technology bonds adjacent shingles together, creating a unified surface that resists wind uplift far better than standard shingles. The HDZ carries a 130 mph wind rating standard — and with the full GAF system, qualifies for GAF's WindProven warranty with no maximum wind speed limit.

The HDZ's SureNail nailing zone is also wider than most competing shingles, making proper installation more consistent. Nail placement errors are the leading cause of premature shingle failure — the larger zone provides meaningful protection against this.

Best for: Most Northeast Ohio residential roofs. The right balance of performance, warranty, and cost.

GAF Timberline UHDZ — Impact-Resistant Upgrade

The UHDZ carries a Class 4 impact resistance rating — the highest available under UL 2218 testing. Class 4 shingles are struck with 2-inch steel balls dropped from 20 feet and must show no cracking. The 2026 hail storm through Solon, Chagrin Falls, and Mayfield Heights produced hailstones up to 2.5 inches — exactly the kind that causes significant damage to standard shingles but minimal damage to Class 4 rated material.

Many Ohio homeowner's insurance companies offer premium discounts of 10–25% for Class 4 roofing. Over the life of the shingle, these savings can offset the higher upfront cost of the UHDZ.

Best for: Hail-prone areas, homeowners who have filed a hail claim before, and those seeking maximum protection and potential insurance savings.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the difference between GAF Timberline NS and HDZ?
    The NS is GAF's entry-level architectural shingle. The HDZ adds LayerLock technology for 130 mph wind resistance plus a wider SureNail nailing zone. For most Northeast Ohio homes the HDZ is the better long-term value and what we recommend as our standard installation.
  • Is the GAF Timberline UHDZ worth it in Ohio?
    Yes for many homeowners. The UHDZ is Class 4 impact resistant — the highest hail resistance rating. Northeast Ohio experiences regular hail and the UHDZ may qualify for a homeowner's insurance discount of 10–25%. Ask your insurer before choosing.
  • What warranty comes with GAF Timberline shingles?
    All Timberline shingles carry a Lifetime limited warranty. When installed by a GAF Certified contractor with the full GAF system, they qualify for the GAF System Plus Limited Warranty — covering both materials and installation workmanship.
  • Which GAF shingle color is most popular in Northeast Ohio?
    Charcoal is the most popular, followed by Weathered Wood and Pewter Gray. These neutrals complement the region's brick colonials, vinyl-sided homes, and craftsman styles. We bring samples to every estimate.
  • How long do GAF Timberline shingles last in Northeast Ohio?
    25–30 years with proper installation and ventilation. Northeast Ohio's freeze-thaw cycles and hail exposure are harder on shingles than milder climates.
