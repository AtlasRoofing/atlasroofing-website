Is your Cleveland commercial roof a candidate for coating?

Roof coatings work best on roofs that have structural integrity but are showing surface degradation — oxidized membranes, minor surface cracking, or small leak points. They're not appropriate for roofs with widespread membrane failure, significant ponding water issues, or compromised insulation. We assess your Cleveland roof and give an honest recommendation on whether coating or replacement makes more sense.

FAQs

Common Questions

What types of roof coatings do you apply?
We apply silicone coatings (best for ponding water resistance and UV stability), elastomeric acrylic coatings (excellent adhesion and flexibility), and polyurethane coatings (high durability) on commercial roofs in Cleveland. The right choice depends on your existing membrane type and performance priorities.
How long does roof coating last?
A professionally applied silicone or elastomeric coating typically lasts 10–15 years and can often be recoated at end of life — extending the value of the original installation. Some manufacturers offer warranties of 10+ years on their coating systems when applied according to specifications.
Is roof coating cheaper than replacement?
Typically 30–50% less expensive than full replacement when the roof is a suitable candidate. For a roof that would otherwise need replacement in 3–5 years but still has solid structure and insulation, coating can be an excellent investment.
Can coating be applied in Ohio's climate?
Most coatings require application above 50°F with no rain in the forecast. In Northeast Ohio, the prime coating season is May through October. We schedule carefully around weather windows to ensure proper cure.
