What Is a Class 4 Impact Resistant Shingle?

A Class 4 shingle is an impact-resistant roofing product that has passed the ANSI/FM 4473 hail impact test using a 2-inch steel ball dropped from a specific height — the highest level of the four-class rating system. Class 4 shingles are significantly more resistant to hail damage than standard shingles. In Northeast Ohio's hail-prone climate, they are worth considering — and many insurance companies offer premium discounts for homes with Class 4 roofing.

The impact resistance rating system

The ANSI/FM 4473 standard tests shingles by dropping steel balls of increasing sizes onto samples and inspecting for cracking. The four classes are:

ClassTest projectileWhat it means
Class 11.25" steel ballBasic — minimum resistance
Class 21.5" steel ballModerate resistance
Class 31.75" steel ballGood resistance
Class 42.0" steel ballMaximum resistance — no cracking

A Class 4 shingle passes the 2-inch impact test with no cracking. The test simulates golf ball-sized hail (1.75") with margin — the same size that caused significant damage in Northeast Ohio during the April 2026 hailstorm.

Is Class 4 worth it in Northeast Ohio?

Northeast Ohio averages 3–5 significant hail events per year. Golf ball-sized hail (1.75") occurs during major storm systems — events that have historically caused widespread roof damage across Cuyahoga, Summit, and Lake counties. For a home that may experience multiple hail events over a 25–30 year roof lifespan, Class 4 shingles provide meaningful protection.

The economics also work. Class 4 shingles cost approximately $800–$1,500 more than standard architectural shingles on a typical Northeast Ohio home. Many insurance companies offer premium discounts of 20–30% for Class 4 installations — discounts that can pay back the upgrade cost within 5–8 years while providing protection for decades.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Class 3 and Class 4 shingles?
Class 3 shingles pass the 1.75-inch steel ball impact test. Class 4 shingles pass the more demanding 2-inch test with no cracking. In practical terms, Class 4 shingles offer meaningfully better resistance to golf ball and larger hail — the size that causes most insurance claims in Northeast Ohio.
Do Class 4 shingles qualify for insurance discounts in Ohio?
Many Ohio homeowners insurance companies offer premium discounts for Class 4 impact-resistant roofing. Discounts vary by insurer and location — call your agent and ask specifically what discount applies at your address before making your shingle selection.
What GAF shingle is Class 4 impact resistant?
The GAF Timberline UHDZ is GAF's Class 4 impact-resistant architectural shingle. It offers the same dimensional appearance and color options as the standard Timberline HDZ with significantly higher hail resistance. Atlas Roofing installs the UHDZ as our impact-resistant upgrade option.
How much more do Class 4 shingles cost?
Class 4 shingles typically add $800–$1,500 to the cost of a standard architectural shingle replacement on a typical Northeast Ohio home, depending on roof size. When weighed against potential insurance premium savings and reduced hail damage risk, the upgrade often has a clear financial case.
Will Class 4 shingles prevent all hail damage?
Class 4 shingles significantly reduce hail damage but do not make a roof immune. Extreme hail events (baseball-sized and larger) can still damage Class 4 shingles. The rating means the shingles resist the 2-inch test ball — most Northeast Ohio hail events involve smaller stones, where Class 4 protection is highly effective.
