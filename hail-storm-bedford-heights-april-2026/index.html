What 2.5" Hail Does to Your Roof

Golf ball-sized hail (2.5 inches) is large enough to cause immediate, significant damage to asphalt shingles — bruising the mat, cracking the surface, and dislodging granules that protect the shingle from UV and moisture. This damage is often completely invisible from the ground but will cause premature roof failure and leaks within 1–3 years if not addressed.

  • Shingle bruising — soft spots where hail impact cracked the mat beneath the granule surface
  • Granule loss — protective coating knocked off, accelerating shingle aging
  • Cracked or fractured shingles — direct splits from large hailstones
  • Dented gutters and downspouts — visible from the ground, sign of roof impact
  • Damaged flashing — metal bent or displaced at chimneys, vents, and valleys
  • Siding and HVAC damage — hail impacts everything exposed on the exterior
