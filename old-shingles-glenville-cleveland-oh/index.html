Signs Your Glenville Home Has Old Shingles

The most common signs of aging shingles we see on Glenville homes from the 1900s–1950s era: curling or cupping along the shingle edges from decades of thermal cycling, significant granule loss leaving the underlying mat exposed, dark streaking from algae growth that accelerates deterioration, widespread cracking from UV exposure and brittleness, and bald spots where wind has removed the granule surface entirely. Any one of these signals a roof that deserves a free inspection — multiple signs together almost always means replacement is the right call.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my shingles are too old in Glenville?+
Signs of aging shingles include: curling or cupping at the edges, loss of granules (dark patches or granules in gutters), cracking or splitting, widespread fading, and shingles that are brittle rather than flexible. Our free inspection documents all of these.
Can old shingles just be repaired?+
Sometimes — if the damage is isolated and the rest of the roof is in good shape, targeted repairs can extend life by several years. But if shingles are over 20 years old and showing widespread wear, full replacement is almost always the better long-term investment. We tell Glenville homeowners honestly which situation they're in.
What happens if I ignore old shingles?+
Old shingles that are past their lifespan lose their ability to shed water effectively. This leads to moisture intrusion, rot, and interior damage that compounds quickly. The cost of waiting is almost always higher than the cost of acting now.
How long does a full roof replacement take?+
Most Glenville homes are completed in a single day — tear-off, inspection, and full installation. We'll give you a clear timeline during your free estimate.
